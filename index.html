Scratch Project: Spot Against the Fairies

Game

The goal of this project was to make a collect to win game. I accomplished this by coding the dog to collect the bone and coding the fairies to try and make the player lose. One challenge included making the fairies move around the stage. I learned that the order of code matters a lot.

Microbit Jukebox Project

This was my jukebox microbit. You could play 1 out of 5 melodies, including nyan cat and happy birthday. The left button played the songs in a set order, while the right button randomly selected a song to play. Text would appear on the microbit, and it would tell you what song is playing. You could also adjust the volume with the blue knob. Throughout this project, I struggled with trying to wire everything properly, but I learned a lot about block coding.

Climate Savers: 2nd Place 2022 Congressional App Challenge

Video Explaining the Game

Our app is called “Climate Savers”, an educational game geared towards young elementary and middle school students to educate them on the effects of climate change and to motivate them to implement sustainable lifestyle choices and to excite them in careers related to the environment so they can someday grow up to be ones who incite change.

Snap Project: Gossiping Sprites

The goal of this project was to get 2 sprites, Abby and Queen Elizabeth II, to talk to each other. I accomplished this by writing different parts of a sentence for them to say to each other. One challenge included having the sprites say the sentences correctly. I learned how to make a sprite choose a phrase out of a random list and how to make custom coding blocks.
